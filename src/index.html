our courses

Introductory, Intermediate, and Advanced Hebrew Language Courses

These courses are designed for students of varying levels and cover the basics of the Hebrew language, including its alphabet, grammar, and vocabulary in the introductory courses. The intermediate courses build on these foundations by introducing more advanced concepts like reading and translating Hebrew texts. Advanced courses cater to students with a solid grasp of the language and offer opportunities to further develop their understanding, including topics such as advanced grammar, syntax, and vocabulary.

Specialized Hebrew Language Courses

These courses offer a deeper dive into specific areas of interest within the study of Biblical Hebrew. They may focus on the use of the language in the Hebrew Bible or in Jewish religious texts, such as the Torah and other Jewish scripture. Additionally, they may cover the use of Hebrew in the study of the life and teachings of Jesus, as presented in the New Testament. These courses provide a unique opportunity for students to gain a more specialized understanding of the role and significance of the Hebrew language in religious studies.

Historical and Cultural Courses on Biblical Hebrew

These courses examine the historical and cultural context of the Hebrew language in ancient Jewish society. They offer an in-depth look at the language's evolution and role in cultural and religious practices. By studying the language in its historical context, students will gain a deeper appreciation for its significance and impact on Judaism, and a broader understanding of ancient Jewish society. The courses may also cover the influence of other cultures on Biblical Hebrew and its evolution.

Hebrew Language Immersion Courses

Hebrew language immersion courses provide a comprehensive and interactive learning experience, fully immersing students in the language and culture. These courses involve activities such as language exchanges and visits to Israel, allowing students to practice and improve their language skills in real-life contexts. The focus is on practical application and cultural immersion, offering a unique and dynamic way to master the Hebrew language.

Jesus, the Torah/Old Testament, and the New Testament in their historical and cultural context

This course covers Jesus, Torah/Old Testament and New Testament in their historical and cultural context. Students will study the life and teachings of Jesus, the sacred texts of Judaism and Christianity, and the background in which they were written. Through lectures, discussions and readings, students will gain deeper understanding of the texts and the religious and cultural traditions they form. Suitable for students interested in religion, history, and cultural studies, regardless of prior knowledge.
